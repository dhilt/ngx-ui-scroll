In addition to get method, settings object can be declared on the Datasource. Here we set bufferSize property which defines minimal number of items that the uiScroll may retrieve via Datasource.get method in a single request. Now the initial load consist of 2 requests of 15 items (from 1 to 15 and from -14 to 0). Initially the uiScroll starts fill the viewport downward from zero scroll position. If the first fetch is enough, the second one will be in the backward direction. The size of these fetches is determined by the bufferSize setting. The next fetches (during scroll) could ask for more than bufferSize items, but never less than bufferSize.

The bigger the bufferSize value is, the less requests generally are needed to fill out the viewport. For example, scrolling from 1 to 100 item when the bufferSize is 1, you may get about 40 Datasource.get calls (depends on the environment). But this demo (where bufferSize is 15) shows that reaching 100 item would cost about 10 Datasource.get calls.

The default value of bufferSize property is 5. The minimum value is 1.
